    Health Score Scale

    90-100: Excellent (Very Healthy)
    Characteristics: Highly nutritious, low in harmful ingredients.
    Examples: Fresh fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains.

    70-89: Good (Balanced)
    Characteristics: Generally healthy, moderate amounts of less desirable nutrients.
    Examples: Low-fat yogurt, nuts, whole grain bread.

    50-69: Average (Moderate Health)
    Characteristics: Higher unhealthy nutrients, some positive elements.
    Examples: Some cereals, processed meats, flavored yogurts.

    30-49: Poor (Unhealthy)
    Characteristics: Nutritionally poor, high unhealthy ingredients.
    Examples: Packaged snacks, sugary drinks, fast food.

    0-29: Very Poor (Highly Unhealthy)
    Characteristics: Heavily processed, low nutritional benefits.
    Examples: Candy, soda, fried foods, junk food.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How do i use ConsumeWise ?

    First, navigate to your profile and edit your details by adding your medical condition and age. Save the changes, then return to the home page. In the chatbox, enter only the product name for the most accurate results. This will provide you with a personalized health analysis based on your health condition.

    What is Health Score?

    A health score is a numerical representation of the nutritional quality of a food product, reflecting its overall healthiness based on its nutrient composition. It helps consumers make informed choices about their diet by assessing how beneficial or harmful a food is to health.
